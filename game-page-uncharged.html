Uncharged

About the game

Games Fleadh

Uncharged was a game I put together in coordination with 3 other classmates. We built it during college under some time constraints to take part in Games Fleadh a game building competition. The game itself was a survival shooter with horror elements.

It was a lot of fun working together with my friends to make something we could all be proud of. We were all from a programming background (besides me; as I enjoy drawing for leasure) so there wasn't really defined roles for each member of the team. We mostly just assembled a list of all the tasks that needed completing and went to work on what we thought suited us best; helping eachother out when we could.

Itch.io

One feature I am particularly proud of was how health ammo and your light radius was calculated. This was a design decision I pitched to the group early on; and everyone seemed thrilled with it.

To explain; In the game your health, ammo and how far you can see, is all governed by the same stat; what little energy you have left. Every shot you fire brings you closer to your end; and the only thing holding off it's inevitable approach is gathering the dying sparks from those who jealously chase your light.

I found that this feature emphasised staying on the move; as staying in one place for too long would lead you to starve yourself of energy. It drove up the tension quite a bit too; since every hit you took; every shot you fired, let you see a little less of the sea of automata marching towards you. Sometimes diving into the hoard becomes the only way to get enough energy to light your way back out again.
