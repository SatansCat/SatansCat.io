About the game

Global Game Jam 2021

Returned was a small game I helped produce in the evenings over a week for the Global Game Jam during 2021. It's core themes centre on hopelessness, and how to overcome feeling like you've lost your way in life. When this happens to our protagonist, they're whisked away to the same place all things that go missing are doomed to end up: Behind the couch cussions!

In this space between spaces a battle forever rages between the Stealie and Unstealie fae, forces responsible for all things lost and found. The protagonist is tasked with finding and returning a number of personal objects to their owners by one of the Unstealie fae; and in doing so, finds themself again through the simple act of helping others.

Itch.io

(I would recomend downloading the game if you wish to play it; the imbedded copy on the Itch.io page is cropped poorly by the website.)

Dispite the rushed and sometimes stressful environment that often comes with partaking in a game jam; this project was fantastic fun to put together. The whole team worked really hard to make sure our finished project still hit home.

My friend Conor and I were the programming team, and one of the things I worked on that I particularly enjoyed was the throw mechanic. I wanted the object to be propelled to the direction the mouse was currently pointing in relation to the player character. My solution in the end was getting a vector pointing from the player to the mouse; normalising it so it would have the same direction but a length I could control and using that to position the object right as you click throw. That way it would already be on the side of the player that it would be thrown from. That way we wouldn't have any problems with the object trying to toss itself through the player and getting stuck.
